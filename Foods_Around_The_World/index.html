China

  • Peking Duck

    Peking Duck is a famous dish from Beijing, known for its crispy skin and tender meat. The duck is seasoned, air-dried, and roasted until the skin is perfectly crispy. It's traditionally served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, and sliced scallions. This dish has a rich history and is often enjoyed during special occasions.

  • Dim Sum

    Dim Sum is a collection of small dishes that are typically served in bamboo steamers. Originating from Cantonese cuisine, dim sum includes a variety of dumplings, buns, rolls, and other bite-sized treats. It's often enjoyed with tea and is a popular choice for brunch. The variety and flavors of dim sum make it a delightful culinary experience2.

  • Kung Pao Chicken

    Kung Pao Chicken is a spicy and flavorful dish from Sichuan cuisine. It consists of diced chicken stir-fried with peanuts, vegetables, and chili peppers in a savory and slightly sweet sauce. The dish is known for its bold flavors and the perfect balance of heat and sweetness, making it a favorite among both locals and visitors2.

Italy

  • Risotto

    Risotto is a creamy and rich rice dish originating from Northern Italy. It's made by slowly cooking Arborio or Carnaroli rice in broth until it reaches a creamy consistency. One of the most famous versions is Risotto alla Milanese, which includes saffron, giving it a distinctive yellow color. This dish is often enriched with Parmesan cheese and butter, making it a luxurious and comforting meal1.

  • Pizza Margherita

    Pizza Margherita is a classic Italian pizza that originated in Naples. It features a thin, crispy crust topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and basil leaves, representing the colors of the Italian flag. This simple yet flavorful pizza is baked in a wood-fired oven, giving it a unique taste and texture. It's a beloved dish worldwide and a symbol of Italian cuisine1.

  • Gelato

    Gelato is Italy's version of ice cream, but it's denser and more flavorful due to its lower air content and higher milk-to-cream ratio. It comes in a variety of flavors, from classic vanilla and chocolate to more adventurous options like pistachio and stracciatella. Gelato is enjoyed year-round in Italy and is a must-try for anyone visiting the country1.

India

  • Biryani

    Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish that is popular across India. It is made by layering basmati rice with marinated meat (such as chicken or lamb), spices, and herbs, then cooking it together to allow the flavors to meld. Each region in India has its own version of biryani, with unique ingredients and cooking methods. It's often served with raita (yogurt sauce) and a boiled egg3.

  • Masala Dosa

    Masala Dosa is a South Indian delicacy made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black lentils). The batter is spread thin on a hot griddle to create a crispy crepe, which is then filled with a spiced potato mixture. It's typically served with coconut chutney and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew). This dish is a popular breakfast item and a staple in South Indian cuisine3.

  • Gulab Jamun

    Gulab Jamun is a beloved Indian dessert made from khoya (reduced milk solids) that is shaped into small balls, deep-fried, and soaked in a sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water. These sweet, soft, and syrupy treats are often enjoyed during festivals and celebrations. They are a perfect way to end a meal on a sweet note3.
